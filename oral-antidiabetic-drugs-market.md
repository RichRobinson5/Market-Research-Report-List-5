<p><p>Oral antidiabetic drugs are medications used to manage blood sugar levels in individuals with diabetes. The market for these drugs includes three main types: Sulfonylureas, which work by increasing the release of insulin from the pancreas; Meglitinides, which also stimulate insulin secretion but have a shorter duration of action than sulfonylureas; Alpha-glucosidase inhibitors, which slow down the absorption of carbohydrates in the intestine. These drugs play a crucial role in the treatment of diabetes by helping to control blood sugar levels.</p></p>
