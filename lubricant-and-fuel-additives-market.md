<p><p>Lubricant and fuel additives can be classified into two main types: single component additives and additive package additives. Single component additives are individual chemicals added to lubricants or fuels to enhance specific properties like viscosity or cleaning abilities. Additive package additives, on the other hand, are combinations of multiple single component additives formulated to provide a comprehensive range of benefits such as improved performance, increased efficiency, and better protection against wear and corrosion. Both types play a crucial role in improving the overall quality and functionality of lubricants and fuels.</p></p>
