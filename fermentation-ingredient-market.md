<p><p>In the fermentation ingredient market, there are two primary types of production processes: batch and continuous. In batch fermentation, ingredients are added to a closed vessel in a specific order and allowed to ferment for a set period before being removed. This method is suitable for small-scale production and allows for more control over the fermentation process. On the other hand, continuous fermentation involves a continuous flow of ingredients through a system, resulting in a more efficient and consistent output, ideal for large-scale production.</p></p>
