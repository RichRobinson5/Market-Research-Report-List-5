<p><p>The port mooring systems market can be classified into two main types based on water depth: shallow and deep/ultra-deep. Shallow mooring systems are designed for water depths below 1000 meters, typically used in ports and harbors with relatively shallow water. On the other hand, deep/ultra-deep mooring systems are for water depths above 1000 meters, often found in offshore drilling rigs and oil platforms in deep-sea environments. Each type of mooring system is specialized to meet the unique challenges posed by different water depths.</p></p>
