<p><p>Hydrogels are commonly used in 2D and 3D cell culture due to their ability to mimic the natural extracellular matrix. There are two main types of hydrogels used in cell culture: natural and synthetic. Natural hydrogels, such as collagen and alginate, are derived from biological sources and closely resemble the native tissue environment. On the other hand, synthetic hydrogels, like polyethylene glycol and polylactic acid, offer more control over physical and chemical properties. Both types play a crucial role in advancing cell culture research and applications.</p></p>
